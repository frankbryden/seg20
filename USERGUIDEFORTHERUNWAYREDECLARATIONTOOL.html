USER GUIDE FOR THE RUNWAY RE-DECLARATION TOOL

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ):

MISCELLANEOUS QUESTIONS:

  1. Why is the page blank when clicking on the “Application” tab?:

If the page is blank, you should select an airport and then a runway by using the “Airport” and “Runway” dropdown buttons. This should give you a front-on and side-on view of the runway you selected.

      2. I cannot find the airport I want in the “Airport” dropdown button:

If you are struggling to find the airport you are working at, you can add a UK airport by clicking on the ”Runway” tab on the right hand side of your screen and then by clicking on the “Add Airport” button. If you need to add a runway to your airport, you can then click on the “Add Runway” button.

      3. Why is there a bell icon at the top-right of the system?

When the user clicks on the bell icon at the top-right of the system, he can check the history of all his previous actions, such as editing or deleting obstacles from the list of obstacles.

      4. What are the four buttons at the bottom-right of the system?:

The four buttons at the bottom-right of your screen allow you to highlight the TODA, LDA, ASDA or TORA on the top-down view according to your need and preference. This allows you to efficiently distinguish one of them when needed.

      5. Where can I see/get the original measurements of the TORA, LDA, ASDA and TODA?

If you are re-calculating the values of the TORA, LDA, ASDA and TODA because an obstacle is present on the runway, you will still be able to see the original values on the left hand side of the recalculated ones in the “Re-declaration” tab.

OBSTACLE RELATED QUESTIONS:

      7. Can I see the height of the obstacles?:

If you need to know the height of an obstacle, you can do so by clicking on the edit button next to the obstacle you choose, in the list of obstacles.

      8. Can I edit the height of an obstacle?:

If you would like to edit the height of an obstacle you added to the list of obstacles or of a predefined obstacle, you can click on the edit button located on the right of the obstacle you would like to edit the height of. Secondly, click on the “Edit details” button and change the height of the obstacle. Once that is done, click on the “Save changes” button to confirm your edit.

      9. Can I rename an obstacle I created?:

If you would like to rename an obstacle you added to the list of obstacles, you can double-click on the obstacle you would like to rename and click on the “Edit details” button and change the name of the obstacle. Once that is done, click on the “Save changes” button to confirm your edit.

TOP-DOWN AND SIDE-ON VIEW RELATED QUESTIONS:

      10. Why is there a red rectangle on the side-on view?:

The red rectangle on the side-on view represents the obstacle you chose to either recalculate the values with or that you added to the view from the “Obstacles” tab.

      11. What does the arrow located on the top-left hand side of the top-down view represent?:

The arrow located on the top-left corner of the top-down view represents a wind direction compass (with respect to the North), which allows the user to visualize the direction of the wind at the specific airport at the specific time the user is using the system. The user is also given the wind speed next to the “Runway” dropdown button.

      12.The colors of the top-down and side-on view bother me. Is there any way to change them?:

If the colors chosen for the top-down and/or side-on view do not suit your personal style or bother you, you have the option to change the colors by clicking on the “View” tab. Once in the “View” tab, click on the color boxes depending on if you’d like to change the top-down or side-on view background color and select your preferred shade. It should automatically change the background color of the chosen view.

      13. What do the green and pink color represent on the different views and why do they appear differently depending on the runway?

The green color you can observe on the top-down and side-on view, represents the clearway while the pink color represents the stopway. Each runway has a different stopway and clearway, therefore the green color and pink color differ for each runway.
